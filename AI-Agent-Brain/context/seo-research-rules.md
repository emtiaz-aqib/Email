# SEO Research Rules

These rules come from the previous ShopLentor article opportunity research skill.

## Purpose

Find and prioritize high-quality websites and existing articles where HasThemes products, especially ShopLentor, can be naturally mentioned, listed, reviewed, or added.

Optimize recommendations for:

- SEO
- AEO
- GEO
- Google SERP visibility

## Research Requirement

Use live web research whenever possible because rankings, article freshness, contact pages, and product lists change frequently.

If current search access is unavailable, clearly say so and provide a reusable research template instead of pretending to have checked live results.

## Prospect Exchange Review Mode

When a prospect suggests a page for exchange collaboration:

1. Review the exact suggested page first.
2. Check product-topic relevance.
3. Check for any banned-niche mention on that page.
4. Check whether the target HasThemes plugin is already listed on that page.
5. Check placement quality and context fit.
6. If weak or non-compliant, find 3-5 better relevant pages from the same domain.
7. Map each suggested page to the most relevant HasThemes plugin.

## Candidate Article Review

For every promising article, inspect:

- Article title and URL
- Article type: listicle, comparison, review, tutorial, resource article, alternatives page
- Relevance to the product
- Whether the article/page includes any mention of banned niches
- Whether the exact target HasThemes plugin is already listed
- Whether ShopLentor/WooLentor is already mentioned
- Which competitors are mentioned
- Whether the product can be added naturally
- Article freshness or last updated date if visible
- Author/editor name if visible
- Contact, advertising, write-for-us, sponsored review, or editorial route
- Best HasThemes plugin match for that page topic

Duplicate listing rule:

- Do not recommend requesting the same plugin if it is already listed on that exact page.
- Recommend either a content-improvement update for the existing listing or another relevant HasThemes plugin not already listed.

## Website Quality Filter

Prioritize websites that:

- Are relevant to WordPress, WooCommerce, eCommerce, SaaS, SEO, web design, or digital marketing.
- Publish plugin listicles, comparisons, tutorials, or product reviews.
- Have visible SERP presence for relevant keywords.
- Appear authoritative, reputable, and non-spammy.
- Are not random multi-niche article farms.
- Have target pages/articles that are clean and free from banned-niche mentions.

Do not recommend low-quality, irrelevant, thin, spammy, casino/adult/crypto-link-farm, or generic guest-post marketplace domains.
Do not recommend an article-level placement if that page mentions banned niches, even when the overall site looks strong.

## Opportunity Scoring

Use a simple score from 1 to 5:

- 5 = Excellent: high authority, ranking/relevant article, competitors listed, product missing or underrepresented, clear contact path.
- 4 = Strong: relevant and good quality, natural fit, contact path exists, but authority/freshness/visibility is slightly weaker.
- 3 = Moderate: relevant but harder contact, lower authority, or less direct topic fit.
- 2 = Low: possible but weak relevance or unclear contact path.
- 1 = Avoid: low quality, spammy, irrelevant, or unnatural placement.

Mandatory score override:

- If the target article/page mentions any banned niche, set score to 1 (Avoid) and do not recommend outreach for that placement.

## Contact Research Priority

Find outreach route in this order:

1. Dedicated sponsored review / advertise / partner page
2. Write for us / contribute page
3. Contact page
4. Author profile with social/email
5. Generic editorial/company email

If no email is visible, provide the contact page instead. Do not invent email addresses.

## Accuracy Rules

- Use citations for claims about article contents, contact pages, dates, and whether products or competitors are mentioned.
- Clearly distinguish verified facts from assumptions.
- Do not claim exact DR, DA, or traffic unless sourced from a live SEO tool or credible public source.
- Prefer current official product and WordPress.org sources for product capabilities.
