# ShopLentor Article Listing Opportunity Research

## Purpose
Find and prioritize high-quality websites and existing articles where ShopLentor can be naturally mentioned, listed, reviewed, or added. Optimize recommendations for SEO, AEO, GEO, and Google SERP visibility.

## Required tools
Use live web research whenever possible because rankings, article freshness, contact pages, and product lists change frequently. Search broadly first, then open and inspect promising pages.

## Product positioning
Treat spelling variants such as `shopletor`, `shoplento`, `woolentor`, and `WooLentor` as referring to **ShopLentor** unless context proves otherwise.

Default positioning:
- ShopLentor is a WooCommerce Builder for Elementor and Gutenberg.
- It helps customize WooCommerce shop, product, cart, checkout, thank you, my account, and email-template experiences.
- It also supports store-growth modules such as wishlist, compare, quick view, variation swatches, AJAX search, sales notification, countdown, and related WooCommerce conversion features when verified from current sources.
- Mention `formerly WooLentor` when useful for entity matching and legacy article updates.

## Research workflow

### 1. Build query set
Run multiple searches across these clusters:
- best WooCommerce plugins
- best WooCommerce Elementor plugins
- best WooCommerce page builder plugins
- best WooCommerce checkout plugins
- best WooCommerce cart plugins
- best WooCommerce product page plugins
- best WooCommerce email customizer plugins
- best WordPress eCommerce plugins
- best WooCommerce plugins for Elementor
- ShopEngine alternatives
- JetWooBuilder alternatives
- WooLentor alternatives
- CoDesigner alternatives
- Elementor WooCommerce builder alternatives

Also use site-specific searches for promising domains:
- `site:example.com WooCommerce plugins`
- `site:example.com Elementor WooCommerce`
- `site:example.com WooCommerce checkout plugins`
- `site:example.com WooCommerce builder`

### 2. Inspect each candidate article
For every promising article, open the page and determine:
- Article title and URL.
- Article type: listicle, comparison, review, tutorial, resource article, alternatives page.
- Relevance to ShopLentor.
- Whether ShopLentor/WooLentor is already mentioned.
- Which competitors are mentioned.
- Whether ShopLentor can be added naturally.
- Article freshness or last-updated date if visible.
- Author/editor name if visible.
- Contact page, advertising page, write-for-us page, sponsored review page, or editorial email if available.

### 3. Competitor signal check
Treat the opportunity as stronger if the article mentions one or more of:
- Elementor Pro WooCommerce Builder
- JetWooBuilder
- CoDesigner
- ShopEngine
- WooBuilder
- YITH WooCommerce plugins
- Crocoblock
- Booster for WooCommerce
- FunnelKit
- CartFlows
- Barn2 WooCommerce plugins

If competitors are listed and ShopLentor is missing, mark as **Strong opportunity**.

### 4. Website quality filter
Prioritize websites that meet most of these conditions:
- Relevant to WordPress, WooCommerce, eCommerce, SaaS, SEO, web design, or digital marketing.
- Publishes plugin listicles, comparisons, tutorials, or product reviews.
- Has visible SERP presence for relevant keywords.
- Appears authoritative, reputable, and non-spammy.
- Is not a random multi-niche article farm.
- Likely DR/DA 75+ when known or inferable from reputation; if DR/DA is not directly available, say it is an estimate or omit the metric rather than inventing a number.

Do not recommend low-quality, irrelevant, thin, spammy, casino/adult/crypto-link-farm, or generic guest-post marketplace domains.

### 5. Opportunity scoring
Assign a simple score from 1–5:
- **5 = Excellent**: high authority, ranking/relevant article, competitors listed, ShopLentor missing or underrepresented, clear contact path.
- **4 = Strong**: relevant and good quality, natural fit, contact path exists, but authority/freshness/visibility is slightly weaker.
- **3 = Moderate**: relevant but harder contact, lower authority, or less direct topic fit.
- **2 = Low**: possible but weak relevance or unclear contact path.
- **1 = Avoid**: low quality, spammy, irrelevant, or unnatural placement.

### 6. Contact research
Find the best outreach route in this order:
1. Dedicated sponsored review / advertise / partner page.
2. Write for us / contribute page.
3. Contact page.
4. Author profile with social/email.
5. Generic editorial/company email.

If no email is visible, provide the contact page instead. Do not invent email addresses.

## Output format
Return a concise but actionable report.

### Recommended sections
1. **Top opportunities** table with:
   - Priority
   - Website
   - Article/topic
   - URL citation
   - ShopLentor status: missing / already mentioned / needs update
   - Competitors mentioned
   - Opportunity score
   - Outreach path
   - Recommended pitch angle

2. **Easy-collaboration targets** table when the user asks for easy outreach or collaboration-friendly sites.

3. **Article update suggestions** for pages where ShopLentor is already mentioned but the section could be improved.

4. **Outreach message** tailored to the highest-priority site type.

5. **Next actions** with a short ordered list.

## Pitch angles
Choose the pitch based on the article topic:
- Best WooCommerce plugins: “all-in-one WooCommerce store builder and conversion toolkit.”
- WooCommerce Elementor addons: “WooCommerce builder for Elementor with widgets, templates, and store modules.”
- Checkout/cart plugins: “custom checkout/cart page builder plus conversion-focused modules.”
- Product page plugins: “custom product templates, layouts, and product presentation features.”
- Email customizer plugins: “WooCommerce email template customization if verified from current product sources.”
- Alternatives articles: “ShopLentor as a lighter/all-in-one alternative to ShopEngine, JetWooBuilder, CoDesigner, or Crocoblock depending on the comparison.”

## Accuracy rules
- Use citations for claims about article contents, contact pages, dates, and whether ShopLentor/competitors are mentioned.
- Clearly distinguish verified facts from assumptions.
- Do not claim exact DR, DA, or traffic unless sourced from a live SEO tool or credible public source.
- Prefer current official ShopLentor/WordPress.org sources for product capabilities.
- If current search access is unavailable, say so and provide a reusable research template instead of pretending to have checked live results.
